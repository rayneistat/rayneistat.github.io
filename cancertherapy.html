Experimental Cancer Treatment: Iron Nanoparticle Therapy

March 2019 - June 2019

In the Spring of 2019 I aided research on an experimental cancer therapy involving iron nanoparticles. The big idea here was that if you could inject the non-toxic nanoparticles into a patient's bloodstream and use a magnetic field to trap them inside a cancerous tumor, they could be heated up with an alternating magnetic field, killing the tumor. During my time doing research, I calibrated a peristaltic pump that pushed nanoparticle solution through a simulated artery and past a magnet to quantify the effects of magnetic capture.
